Complex Filters

Overview​

Complex filters combine multiple conditions using logical operators (AND, OR) to create sophisticated queries. This page covers:

  • AND/OR logical operators
  • Nested conditions with parentheses
  • text search functions for partial matching
  • IN operator for multiple values
  • Performance optimization for complex filters

AND Operator​

Multiple AND Conditions​

Filter active products in Electronics category:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=XPRD06 eq true AND XPRD05 eq 'Electronics'

Generated SQL:

WHERE XPRD06 = 1
  AND XPRD05 = 'Electronics'
  AND XPRD01_AMBIENTE IN (...)
  AND TREC != 'C'

Three or More AND Conditions​

Filter by category, price range, and status:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' AND XPRD02 gt 100 AND XPRD02 lt 500 AND XPRD06 eq true

Generated SQL:

WHERE XPRD05 = 'Electronics'
  AND XPRD02 > 100
  AND XPRD02 < 500
  AND XPRD06 = 1

Result: Electronics products priced between $100-$500 that are active.

OR Operator​

Simple OR Condition​

Find products in Electronics OR Toys category:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 eq 'Toys'

Generated SQL:

WHERE (XPRD05 = 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 = 'Toys')
  AND XPRD01_AMBIENTE IN (...)
  AND TREC != 'C'

Multiple OR Conditions​

Find products with multiple status values:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=XPRD13 eq 'active' OR XPRD13 eq 'pending' OR XPRD13 eq 'review'

Better alternative: Use IN operator (see below).

Combining AND/OR​

OR with AND​

Find active products in either Electronics or Toys:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=(XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 eq 'Toys') AND XPRD06 eq true

Generated SQL:

WHERE (XPRD05 = 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 = 'Toys')
  AND XPRD06 = 1

Parentheses are critical - they control evaluation order.

Complex Example​

Find high-value products in specific categories:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=(XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 eq 'Appliances') AND XPRD02 gt 500 AND XPRD06 eq true

What it does:

  1. Category must be Electronics OR Appliances
  2. AND price must be > $500
  3. AND product must be active

Text Search Functions​

contains() - Search Text​

Find products with "Widget" in name:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=contains(XPRD01,'Widget')

Generated SQL:

WHERE contains(XPRD01,'Widget')

Matches:

  • "Premium Widget"
  • "Widget Pro"
  • "Super Widget 2000"

startwith() - Starts With​

Find products starting with "Pro":

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=startwith(XPRD01,'Pro')

Matches:

  • "Pro Widget"
  • "Professional Tool"
  • "Product X"

endwith() - Ends With​

Find products ending with "2025":

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=endwith(XPRD03,'2025')

Matches:

  • "PRD-001-2025"
  • "WDG-2025"

Text Search with AND​

Search active products with specific name:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=contains(XPRD01,'Widget') AND XPRD06 eq true

Case Sensitivity​

Text search functions are case-insensitive by default (MySQL):

$filter=contains(XPRD01,'widget')
# Matches: "Widget", "WIDGET", "widget"

For case-sensitive search (Oracle):

  • Depends on database collation
  • May require LOWER() function in custom queries

IN Operator​

Multiple Values​

Find products in multiple categories:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=XPRD05 in ('Electronics','Toys','Appliances')

Generated SQL:

WHERE XPRD05 IN ('Electronics', 'Toys', 'Appliances')

More efficient than:

# ❌ Less efficient
$filter=XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 eq 'Toys' OR XPRD05 eq 'Appliances'

IN with Numbers​

Find products with specific IDs:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=PRD_ID in (123,456,789)

IN with AND​

Find specific products that are active:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=PRD_ID in (123,456,789) AND XPRD06 eq true

Nested Conditions​

Grouping with Parentheses​

Complex business logic:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=(XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' AND XPRD02 gt 1000) OR (XPRD05 eq 'Jewelry' AND XPRD02 gt 500)

What it does:

  • (Electronics products > $1000) OR
  • (Jewelry products > $500)

Generated SQL:

WHERE (XPRD05 = 'Electronics' AND XPRD02 > 1000)
   OR (XPRD05 = 'Jewelry' AND XPRD02 > 500)

Three-Level Nesting​

Advanced filtering:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=((XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 eq 'Computers') AND XPRD02 gt 500) OR (XPRD30 eq true AND XPRD02 gt 100)

Logic:

  • ((Electronics OR Computers) AND price > $500) OR
  • (Featured products AND price > $100)

⚠️ Warning: Deep nesting can hurt performance and readability.

Range Queries​

Between (Using AND)​

Find products priced between $100-$500:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=XPRD02 ge 100 AND XPRD02 le 500

Generated SQL:

WHERE XPRD02 >= 100 AND XPRD02 <= 500

Date Ranges​

Find orders created in December 2025:

GET /api/v4/core/ORD?source=orderList&\
  $filter=CDATA ge '20251201000000' AND CDATA le '20251231235959'

Note: CDATA format is YmdHis (14 characters).

Exclude Range (Using OR)​

Find products under $50 OR over $500:

GET /api/v4/core/PRD?source=productList&\
  $filter=XPRD02 lt 50 OR XPRD02 gt 500

Performance Optimization​

Index Usage​

✅ Good - Uses index on XPRD05:

$filter=XPRD05 eq 'Electronics'

❌ Bad - Can't use index efficiently:

$filter=contains(XPRD01,'Widget')  # Leading wildcard

✅ Better - Can use index:

$filter=startwith(XPRD01,'Widget')  # No leading wildcard

AND Before OR​

✅ Good - AND filters first (reduces result set):

$filter=XPRD06 eq true AND (XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 eq 'Toys')

❌ Less efficient - OR first (larger intermediate result):

$filter=(XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' OR XPRD05 eq 'Toys') AND XPRD06 eq true

Note: Modern query optimizers handle this, but explicit AND-first is clearer.

Avoid Complex OR​

❌ Slow - Many OR conditions:

$filter=XPRD05 eq 'A' OR XPRD05 eq 'B' OR ... OR XPRD05 eq 'Z'

✅ Fast - Use IN:

$filter=XPRD05 in ('A','B',...,'Z')

Limit OR Depth​

❌ Bad - Deep nesting:

$filter=((A OR B) AND (C OR D)) OR ((E OR F) AND (G OR H))

✅ Better - Split into multiple simpler queries:

// Query 1: (A OR B) AND (C OR D)
// Query 2: (E OR F) AND (G OR H)
// Merge results client-side

JavaScript Examples​

Complex Filter Builder​

class FilterBuilder {
  constructor() {
    this.conditions = [];
  }

  add(field, operator, value) {
    if (typeof value === 'string') {
      this.conditions.push(`${field} ${operator} '${value}'`);
    } else {
      this.conditions.push(`${field} ${operator} ${value}`);
    }
    return this;
  }

  and() {
    return this;
  }

  or() {
    // Mark last condition for OR grouping
    this.conditions[this.conditions.length - 1] += ' OR';
    return this;
  }

  build() {
    return this.conditions.join(' AND ').replace(/ OR AND /g, ' OR ');
  }
}

// Usage
const filter = new FilterBuilder()
  .add('XPRD05', 'eq', 'Electronics')
  .and()
  .add('XPRD02', 'gt', 100)
  .and()
  .add('XPRD06', 'eq', true)
  .build();

// Result: "XPRD05 eq 'Electronics' AND XPRD02 gt 100 AND XPRD06 eq true"

Dynamic Search​

async function searchProducts(searchCriteria) {
  const filters = [];

  // Add category filter
  if (searchCriteria.categories && searchCriteria.categories.length) {
    const categoryList = searchCriteria.categories.map(c => `'${c}'`).join(',');
    filters.push(`XPRD05 in (${categoryList})`);
  }

  // Add price range
  if (searchCriteria.minPrice) {
    filters.push(`XPRD02 ge ${searchCriteria.minPrice}`);
  }
  if (searchCriteria.maxPrice) {
    filters.push(`XPRD02 le ${searchCriteria.maxPrice}`);
  }

  // Add text search
  if (searchCriteria.searchText) {
    filters.push(`contains(XPRD01,'${searchCriteria.searchText}')`);
  }

  // Add active filter
  filters.push('XPRD06 eq true');

  // Combine with AND
  const $filter = filters.join(' AND ');

  const url = new URL(`${apiBase}/api/v4/core/PRD`);
  url.searchParams.append('source', 'productList');
  url.searchParams.append('$filter', $filter);

  const response = await fetch(url, {
    headers: { 'Authorization': `Bearer ${token}` }
  });

  return response.json();
}

// Usage
const results = await searchProducts({
  categories: ['Electronics', 'Computers'],
  minPrice: 100,
  maxPrice: 500,
  searchText: 'Pro'
});

Best Practices​

✅ DO:​

Use IN for multiple values:

$filter=XPRD05 in ('Electronics','Toys')

Group OR conditions with parentheses:

$filter=(A OR B) AND C

Use indexed fields in filters:

$filter=XPRD03 eq 'PRD-2025-001'  # XPRD03 is counter (indexed)

Avoid leading wildcards in LIKE:

$filter=startwith(XPRD01,'Widget')  # ✅ Can use index

❌ DON'T:​

Don't use multiple OR when IN works:

# ❌ Bad
$filter=A eq 'X' OR A eq 'Y' OR A eq 'Z'

# ✅ Good
$filter=A in ('X','Y','Z')

Don't nest too deeply:

# ❌ Bad - hard to read and maintain
$filter=(((A OR B) AND (C OR D)) OR ((E OR F) AND (G OR H)))

Don't use LIKE with leading wildcard on large tables:

# ❌ Slow on 100k+ rows
$filter=contains(XPRD04,'text')

Common Mistakes​

Mistake 1: Missing Parentheses​

# ❌ Wrong - unexpected precedence
$filter=A eq 1 OR B eq 2 AND C eq 3

# Evaluates as: A eq 1 OR (B eq 2 AND C eq 3)

# ✅ Correct - explicit grouping
$filter=(A eq 1 OR B eq 2) AND C eq 3

Mistake 2: String Quoting in IN​

# ❌ Wrong
$filter=XPRD05 in (Electronics,Toys)

# ✅ Correct
$filter=XPRD05 in ('Electronics','Toys')

Mistake 3: Operator Precedence​

AND has higher precedence than OR:

# This filter:
A OR B AND C

# Evaluates as:
A OR (B AND C)

# Not as:
(A OR B) AND C

# Always use parentheses!

Summary​

Complex filters enable sophisticated queries:

  • ✅ AND combines multiple required conditions
  • ✅ OR matches any of multiple conditions
  • ✅ LIKE enables partial text matching
  • ✅ IN efficiently checks multiple values
  • ✅ Parentheses control evaluation order
  • ✅ Performance depends on indexes and filter structure

Key Takeaways:

  1. Use parentheses to make logic explicit
  2. Prefer IN over multiple OR conditions
  3. Put AND conditions before OR for better performance
  4. Avoid leading wildcards in LIKE (%)
  5. Limit nesting depth for maintainability
